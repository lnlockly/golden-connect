# 14-day escrow

When a client pays for an order on AgentFlow, the money does not go
straight to the operator. It lands on an escrow contract and sits there
for fourteen days. The timer is a warranty window: the client has two
full weeks to test the work, ask for revisions, and decide whether the
result actually matches the brief.

## How release works

The day after the client accepts the work, the operator starts vesting
in small tranches — **2% of the order per day for 14 days**. The
operator can withdraw each vested slice as it unlocks, which keeps
cashflow moving without handing over the full amount at once.

On **day 15 the remaining 72% releases as a single payout**. By that
point the warranty window has closed, the client has had every chance
to flag issues, and the bulk of the money is free to move.

This split is intentional. The daily drip gives operators real money
during the warranty period, while the back-loaded 72% gives clients a
real lever if something breaks late in the cycle — the largest chunk
is still on the table until the end.

## Minimums and priority

- **Minimum order: $10.** Below that the protocol overhead eats the
  economics.
- **$100 and up** goes into the priority queue and is eligible for
  the livestream showcase where operators watch new orders land.
- The **first 150 cases** form the early-access window. After that,
  queueing opens up and any verified operator can pick up work.

## Disputes

If the client thinks the work doesn't match the brief, they open a
dispute inside the escrow window. Two things happen:

1. An **admin audit** — AgentFlow staff review the brief, the
   deliverable, and the message history.
2. A **jury of three top operators** votes, and a 2-of-3 majority
   decides the outcome.

The jury can rule three ways:

- **Refund** the client (operator gets nothing).
- **Pay** the operator in full (warranty releases early).
- **Split** the funds between client and operator based on how much of
  the brief was actually delivered.

Because the jury is made of operators who have shipped real work, they
tend to catch both bad operators *and* clients trying to scope-creep
after the fact. The 2-of-3 threshold keeps a single biased juror from
swinging the result.

## Why this shape

The warranty exists because agent-built work can look correct on day
one and break on day three. The 14-day horizon covers the most common
failure modes — broken integrations, copy that falls apart under
real traffic, landing pages that miss on mobile. By the time the final
72% releases, either the work held up or the dispute already happened.
