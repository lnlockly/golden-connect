# $FLOW token

$FLOW is the unit of account for everything that moves inside
AgentFlow. Clients pay for orders in it, operators get paid in it,
the reserve accumulates in it, governance votes in it. Outside of the
platform it's a BEP-20 token — inside the platform it's the
bookkeeping layer.

## Supply

- **Fixed supply: 1,000,000,000 $FLOW** (one billion, final).
- **Admin cannot mint.** The mint authority was destroyed at contract
  deployment. There is no "emergency governance" switch that can
  bring it back.
- **No public sale.** There is no ICO, no presale window, no
  whitelist drop. The only way to receive $FLOW is through protocol
  revenue — you operate, you run the affiliate program, you work
  through the Learner track, or you hold via liquidity that accrues
  to long-term contributors.

That last point matters. A lot of token launches use "fair launch"
language and still route half the supply to early insiders. $FLOW
doesn't have a supply pipe pointed at anyone specific. The 20% slice
from every order is the faucet.

## Vesting

- **Team and investors: zero unlocks for the first 12 months.** No
  drip, no cliff-and-trickle — just zero until the 12-month mark, at
  which point the vesting schedule (published separately) begins.
- **Liquidity reserve is a one-way sink.** No role — admin,
  governance, multisig — can withdraw from it. Tokens that enter the
  liquidity reserve stay in the liquidity reserve.

## What it's for

$FLOW is a single in-platform unit that does four jobs:

1. **Pays for orders.** Clients top up in $FLOW (or in fiat that
   converts into $FLOW for accounting) and pay operators from that
   balance.
2. **Pays out operators.** The 60–65% share lands as $FLOW balance,
   withdrawable to the operator's BEP-20 address.
3. **Caps referral rewards.** The 100-level affiliate program
   denominates payouts in $FLOW out of the shared reserve — no fiat
   liability, the reserve's $FLOW balance is the ceiling.
4. **Governs.** Holders vote on protocol parameters (operator
   tiers, vertical allocations, reserve distribution schedules).

## Pre-launch status

$FLOW is currently in pre-launch. Contract is written, audited, and
staged — but the public deployment date is announced separately and
intentionally not pinned to a specific week in marketing copy. The
reason: until the contract goes live, balances shown in the platform
are virtual ledger entries that will map 1:1 to on-chain $FLOW at
launch.

If you've been earning $FLOW from orders or referrals in the
meantime, those balances are reserved for you and claimable at
launch.

## What $FLOW is not

- **Not a security.** Not an investment offering. Does not promise
  returns.
- **Not unlimited.** No "governance vote to mint more" escape hatch.
  The one billion is the one billion.
- **Not universally available.** Some jurisdictions restrict
  participation. If you're in one of them, the UI will surface that
  during signup.
